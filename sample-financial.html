Financial writing

In finance, it is important for your clients to feel that they are being looked after by real people who genuinely care, and who have the know-how to guide them to success.

To reinforce this impression with your writing, make sure that the subjects of your sentences are human beings — instead of abstract concepts — who are actively doing real, tangible things. Avoid loose, overlong sentences and don’t shy from the human element in your writing. Take responsibility with the active voice and be accurate and direct with your language; try to avoid nominalised verbs and frill.

Below is an excerpt from “Bennett Financial”; we can improve it significantly.

ORIGINAL VERSION:

Our business is primarily multi-national in nature, reflecting our broad geographic reach. Bennett Financial interacts significantly with advisory teams located both inside and outside the Canadian borders, in both developed and emerging markets, in handling projects. Bennett Financial’s extensive market knowledge and efficient execution in each region is its significant strategic differentiator.

Combining the advantages of personalized service with access to the resources of a multi-national investment firm, we are the favourite choice of discerning Canadian investors.

New VERSION:

Bennett Financial manages the finances of clientele all over the world. Due to our global reach, we are able to work closely with advisory teams both inside and outside Canadian borders, in both developed and emerging markets. This affords us deep and up-to-the-minute market knowledge which, coupled with efficient execution, stands as our principal strategic advantage.

By combining the personalized service of a boutique broker with the resources of a multi-national investment firm, we are the favourite choice for the discerning Canadian investor.
